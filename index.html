UCL

Mathematics MSc

Key Info: 2:1 first year (69%), second year 2:1 (69%)

Analysis: 1, 2, 3, 4; Newtonian Mechanics, Probablity and Stats, Methods: 1, 2, 3, 4; Fluid mechanics, Algebra: 1, 2, 3; Computation Methods and Programming

Analysis 1, 2, 3, 4: Proving basic results about series in Analysis 1 and 2 along with key results about differentiation such as smooth and continuous functions are differentiable. Furthermore we learnt about results from Riemann integrablity and radii of convergence of series. In analysis 3 we continued with complex analysis such as Cauchy's integral formula and various other remarkable results which have applications in applied maths. In Analysis 4 we talked more general topological spaces such as metric spaces and hausdorff spaces. This will be continued in measure theory when measure spaces are introduced.

Methods 1, 2, 3, 4: Applied maths in action. Methods 1 was a recap of A-level type maths. Methods 2 introduced vector calculus with grad and curl operators along with surface and triple integrals to name a few. Methods 3 showed us how to use fourier series and half Fourier series to solve the wave equation. Methods 4 introduced Fourier transforms and Laplace transforms as well as Bessels equation equation and the seperated solutions used to solve it using the Frobenius Anzats.

Algebra 1, 2, 3: Key overarching concept LINEAR ALGEBRA. Fundamental concept for solving multiple equations, similarly vectorspace and kernel introduced. Abstraction of vectorspace's continued. Alegbra 2 included Euclid's algorithm which allows you to find the HCF of two numbers. Number theory with automorphisms supplied the rest of the module. Alebra 3 included inner product spaces, dual spaces, Jordan normal form and Bezout's Lemma.

Probablity and Stats contained distributions such as Gamma and Beta. It also showed joint distributions and conditional expectation involving more than one random variable. Computational Methods taught numerical analysis techniques such as QR decomposition, LU decomposition and one of the most important ones SVD. Many many more were included.
